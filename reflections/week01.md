# Week 1 Reflection

Reflect 1: What counts as “making” in the age of AI? What might be gained/lost when machines participate in making?

Making is the process by which humans create or produce something using their ideas and effort. In the context of AI, this definition gets tricky because humans are not always doing all of the creating anymore. Instead of making something entirely by hand, people might guide AI by giving prompts, ideas, or feedback. The human is still involved, but the machine is doing part of the work. This makes us question whether making is about actually producing something, or about making choices and directing the process. When machines participate in making, a lot can be gained, like saving time, increasing efficiency, and helping people create things they might not be able to make on their own. AI can also spark new ideas and make creative work more accessible. However, something can be lost too, such as human creativity, effort, and personal expression. People may rely too much on machines, and the work might feel less meaningful or less personal.
