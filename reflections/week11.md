# Week 11 Reflection

Reflect 11: Who does the invisible work of AI, and who profits from it?

The invisible work of AI is carried out by people around the world, often working in flexible, temporary, or contract roles that support systems like content moderation, data labeling, mining, and transcription. These workers are often hidden, even though their labor is essential to making AI systems function. For example, in my research, I learned about “ghost workers,” who complete small tasks that help train AI systems. They are often students, parents, or part-time workers who are underpaid and not recognized.

Most of the profit goes to large tech companies like Amazon, Google, and Microsoft, creating an imbalance where many people do the work, but a few companies benefit and profit the most.
