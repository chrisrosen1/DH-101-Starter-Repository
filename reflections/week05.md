# Week 5 Reflection

Reflect 5: How does AI alter authorship and remix culture? Who owns AI-made art?

AI changes what authorship even means. Before AI, if you made something, it was clearly yours because you created it yourself. Now, when you use AI, it feels more like a collaboration. The AI generates the content, but it only does that because of the prompts you give it. The final product comes from your ideas and the system’s output. That makes authorship feel more shared and a little blurry.

With AI and remix culture, ownership is also more complicated. The original artwork still belongs to the creator. But when someone builds on that work and changes it using AI, something new is created very quickly. AI makes remixing faster and easier, which adds another layer to ownership. Now there can be three parts involved: the original owner, the AI system, and the person guiding it through prompts. Because of this, it becomes harder to clearly say who fully owns the final result.
