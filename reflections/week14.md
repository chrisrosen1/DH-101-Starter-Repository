# Week 14 Reflection

Reflect 14: What does it mean to be human in the age of AI?

At the beginning of this course, I saw AI as just a tool that could do everything for you. I thought it could generate ideas, create content, and take over most of the work. But over time, I learned that AI cannot do things on its own. It still needs human input, direction, and decisions at every stage.

AI can produce text, images, and ideas very quickly. But a lot of the time, it feels repetitive or lacks real depth. It can copy human creativity, but it does not actually experience anything. It does not have emotions or personal understanding. I saw this in my projects, especially when I created the AI therapy transcript. It sounded real, but something felt off. That showed me that being human is not just about producing content. It is about feeling, interpreting, and making meaning.

I also learned that humans are still a key part of the creative process. AI can help, but it cannot replace human creativity. Humans make the choices that make something unique. We decide what to include, how it should look, and what it should mean. Without those decisions, AI work would feel generic.

The course also made me think about the systems behind AI. There is hidden human labor and real environmental impact. This showed me that AI is not neutral and comes with responsibility.

Overall, I do not think the debate is really about humans vs. AI. AI depends on humans and cannot replace what makes us human. Being human in the age of AI is about understanding our role in using these tools. AI is powerful, but it still needs human creativity and decisions. What makes us human is how we think, feel, and create meaning, and that is something AI cannot replace.
