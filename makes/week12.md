# Week 12 – AI & Ecology

## The Artifact
This infographic reveals the hidden environmental impact of AI by breaking down the energy and carbon costs behind systems like GPT-3. By using comparisons like flights and cars, it makes these abstract numbers more concrete and relatable.

## Reflection
A big part of this project for me is realizing that even though I understand the environmental impact of AI, I’m not sure how much it will actually change my behavior. I can see how much energy AI uses and how it adds up at a large scale, but it’s hard to connect that to my own use. My individual impact feels so small that it’s difficult to believe that changing my habits would make any real difference.
That’s what makes this problem frustrating. It depends on millions of people making small changes, but no single person feels like they matter enough to make a dent. Because of that, it’s easy to keep using AI the same way, even when you know there are environmental costs behind it. I don’t think I will stop using AI, especially because it is so useful for school and everyday tasks.
At the same time, this project made me more aware of how often I use it without thinking. Even if my changes are small, I can still try to be more intentional, like not using AI for things I can easily do myself. I don’t think individual action solves the problem, but it’s still part of it. The real challenge is that this only works if a lot of people start thinking the same way.
