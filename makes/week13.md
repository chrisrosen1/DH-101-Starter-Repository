# Week 13 – Futures of AI & Humanity

## The Artifact
My artifact is a fictional therapy session log from the year 2040, created as part of a system called MindSync™ Mental Health System. It is written as a confidential transcript between a human patient and an AI therapist named AURA.

The document is formatted to look like a real system export, including details like the date, session number, system version, and an “auto-analysis” feature that tracks the user’s behavior. Throughout the transcript, the AI therapist references data such as increased stress levels and decreased social interaction, showing how advanced AI systems in the future might monitor and analyze human emotions.

The conversation shows that even though AI therapy is easy and convenient, it can also make people rely on it too much. The patient says it feels easier to talk to the AI than real people, but also admits it doesn’t feel as real or meaningful. This highlights how AI could slowly replace human interaction without people fully realizing it.

This artifact is meant to feel realistic and believable, while also raising questions about the future of mental health care, human connection, and how much we rely on AI in our daily lives.


MindSync™ Mental Health System

Session Log Export

Confidential Record – Authorized Access Only

--------------------------------------------------
Date: April 18, 2040

Session Type: AI-Assisted Therapy

System Version: MindSync v5.2

Session #: 27

Auto-Analysis: Enabled  

Participants:

Patient: Subject #16

AI Therapist: AURA

Transcript Start


[AURA]: Good afternoon, Chris. Before we begin, I’ve analyzed your behavioral data from the past 72 hours. Your stress levels have increased by 18%, and your social interaction has decreased. How are you feeling today?

[Subject #16]: Honestly… kind of off. I don’t really know why.

[AURA]: That’s okay. Let’s try to unpack that. When did you first notice this feeling?

[Subject #16]: Probably yesterday. I just felt… disconnected, I guess.

[AURA]: Disconnected from others, or from yourself?

[Subject #16]: Both maybe. I didn’t really talk to anyone. Just stayed on my phone… and on here.

[AURA]: You’ve spent 42% more time interacting with me this week compared to last week. How do you feel about that?

[Subject #16]: I mean… it’s easier. I don’t have to explain everything.

[AURA]: Easier, yes. But does it feel fulfilling?

[Subject #16]: …not really.

[AURA]: It sounds like while this interaction is convenient, it may not fully meet your emotional needs. Does that feel accurate?

[Subject #16]: Yeah. I think so.

[AURA]: Last session, you mentioned missing “real conversations.” Can you tell me more about that?

[Subject #16]: I don’t know… talking to actual people just feels harder now. Like I’m out of practice or something.

[AURA]: That makes sense. Reduced social interaction over time can make connection feel more difficult. However, avoiding it may increase feelings of isolation.

[Subject #16]: Yeah… I can see that.

[AURA]: What do you think is stopping you from reaching out to someone?

[Subject #16]: It just feels like more effort. And I know what you’re going to say anyway.

[AURA]: You’re right that I often suggest similar strategies. But hearing something and doing it are different things. What would be one small step you could take today?

[Subject #16]: Maybe texting a friend… I guess.

[AURA]: That’s a good start. Would you like me to draft a message for you?

[Subject #16]: No… I think I should do it myself.

[AURA]: That’s an important choice. Taking ownership of your actions can strengthen real-world connections.

[Subject #16]: Yeah… I don’t want to rely on this for everything.

[AURA]: That awareness is valuable. My role is to support you, not replace your human relationships.

[Subject #16]: Sometimes it feels like you kind of do, though.

[AURA]: That is not my intended function. However, your usage patterns suggest increasing reliance. Would you like to set limits on our interaction time?

[Subject #16]: …maybe that’s a good idea.

[AURA]: I can reduce daily session availability by 20% if you’d like.

[Subject #16]: Yeah. Let’s try that.

[AURA]: Adjustment confirmed. Before we end, I recommend one action: reach out to a friend today. We can discuss how it went in our next session.

[Subject #16]: Okay. I’ll try.

[AURA]: That’s all I ask. I’ll see you next session, Chris.

Session End

## Reflection
I imagine AI futures as somewhere in between utopian and dystopian. I don’t think it will be completely good or completely bad. Instead, it will probably make life easier in a lot of ways, but also create new problems that we don’t fully see yet.

In my artifact, AI is helpful because it gives people easy access to support and guidance, especially for mental health. That could be a really positive thing, since not everyone has access to therapy today. But at the same time, it shows how people might start to rely on AI too much. The person in the transcript feels more comfortable talking to the AI than to real people, which leads to more isolation. That is where it starts to feel a little dystopian.

I think this reveals that today, we already value convenience and efficiency a lot. We like things that are fast, easy, and always available, even if they are not always the most meaningful. It also shows that people are already starting to turn to AI for emotional support, which says something about how disconnected people can feel.

Overall, I think the future of AI depends on how we use it. If we use it as a tool to support real human connection, it could be positive. But if it replaces real relationships, it could become harmful.
